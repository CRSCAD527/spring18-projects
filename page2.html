Hiding in Plain Sight: Exploring Social Vulnerability in Massachusetts Towns

The 351 town and city communities of Massachusetts contain over 6 million people spread from Boston to the Berkshires. Given the rapid pace of climate change, vulnerable populations in all local geographies will likely facing increasing risk. This project focuses on three different types of vulnerability-poverty; adults over 65 years old; and disability. Using 2016 American Community Survey data, a vulnerability index was developed across all three variables, resulting in composite scores and thematic mapping that show social vulnerability across the state.

A spatial analysis of coastal flooding in Bangladesh

The 4th Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) report highlights the increased vulnerability of the coastal areas from floods due to sea-level rise (SLR). Bangladesh is the eighth most populous country in the world, and is disproportionately affected by climate change. The coastal area of Bangladesh accounts for 32 percent of total land area and hosts 25.7 percent of total population. The primary aim of this project was to calculate the difference of coastal erosion in the 19 coastal districts of Bangladesh over an 8-year period (2010 and 2018).

Measuring educational attainment in proximity to higher education institutions throughout Massachusetts

One, three, and five-mile fixed distance buffers around each college/university location were utilized as catchments to develop census statistical zones for comparative analysis.
